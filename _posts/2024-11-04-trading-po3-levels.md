## How PO3 Levels + Time Compare to CRT
CRT is great but it has a few limitations that make it difficult to find good setups. The first limitation is the defined candle range. Hancock’s Model uses H4 candles so it is a 4 H1 candle range. I’ve seen that sticking to this strictly is not always accurate. Sometimes the 5th candle away has a slightly longer wick, or sometimes only the last 2 candles are relevant for a range. In other instances the range is 8 hours long. After going through the idea of a 911 Model, I’ve realized that CRT doesn’t represent accurate ranges. You could introduce additional timings like 5AM, or you could choose to use H1 candles (I’ve seen some use the exotic 2H candle), but will this be sufficient to represent a range suitable for price action or for liquidity? When backtesting the 911 Model I even thought of creating custom EAs to mark a custom range for 9 hours or 6 hours on the same chart.

The second issue is key timing. 9AM is by far the best timing for volume and movement because it is the US market’s opening hour. Using CRT, apart from 9AM, typical H4 timings are chosen. These are 5AM, 1PM, 5PM, 9PM, and 1AM. These timings however do not always provide a setup. I’ve seen reversals begin at 8AM or at 2AM etc. If you choose to trade H1 candles, then you can use any timing during the day. But again, the CRT model needs to be defined - timing, range definition, entry criteria, etc. You could be watching candles all day, or you could be looking at just the 9AM candle. It is literally a 24-hour window if you don’t state your chosen timing.

Now when I review PO3 Levels + Time trading, I see a very simple model. Levels are marked and noted according to their strength. The higher the PO3 number the higher the strength. So a 729 PO3 level will be stronger than a 243 PO3 level. In my markings, 81 PO3 is the lowest and it marks the partitions in between. It is also the level that price tends to move within. On the few occasions that price quickly trades through  three 81 PO3 levels, I know there will be big moves, either in retracement or impulse moves, as we are now expanding and moving in larger PO3 factor levels. Later on, in consolidation, price contracts and trades in the lowest PO3 level. 

When price trades to a high PO3 number I am careful and observe for a while. There can be raids at times and price then moves in the opposite direction. But for price to trade through the lower PO3 numbers, it is clear. Price moves from Discount to Equilibrium, then could either continue into Premium or retrace back. The same vice versa. This predictability is what makes PO3 levels very accurate and allows for a high win rate.

The times I’ve seen today have been the odd number timings after 1AM. An opportunity formed at 1AM, 3AM, then 5AM, and 7AM. I will be trading 9AM as my main entry for the day. It looks like from the Asia session onwards, setups can form if price is willing to move. We could trade these small moves from Asia, London, into New York for the main trade. But again, this is just Monday, we don’t know how tomorrow will move. It is also US Election Day so best to stay out of the market.

One thing I’m also noticing is that it is better to trade at or near a PO3 level. If you get the direction wrong, quickly close the trade and enter in the opposite trade. Stop losses also will be easy to set up and limit too much movement. Beyond the PO3 level, the trade is invalid if it goes more than a third of the partition. Liquidity runs tend to be one PO3 order down. So for a 81 PO3 move, the stop run will be 27 pips.

## Similar to Hougaard Price Action Trading
There was a time when I traded pure price action on m1. It was great for learning how to cut losses as soon as price went against me. But I was new that time, it was an elementary phase I was passing through. I never went back to trading that way. I thought it was too simplistic, too basic, an unintelligent trading method.

But looking at PO3 level trading now. It really appears to be the same thing, except i know confidently where price may reverse, or retrace for a short while. The trade execution though is the same. We look for a csd or a turtle soup or doji formation to signal reversal. Then we close the trade reaching this level and open a reversal trade. It is simple. If it is a middle level, we watch for continuation.
