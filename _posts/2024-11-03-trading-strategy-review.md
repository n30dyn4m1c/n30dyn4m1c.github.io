## General CRT
The original theory of candle ranges was a basic three candle pattern. The first formed the dynamic range, the second was a manipulation candle which contained a raid, a turtle soup, then the third was the distribution or expansion candle back into the first candle’s range.

I spent probably two weeks backtesting and live trading this concept. Many times I traded it on a daily candle timeframe, other times on the 1 hour and 4 hour time frames. It was during this time that ICT himself posted on X and discredited CRT. Nevertheless, Romeo rebutted and the community still went on.

I may have quit CRT for a moment there. But I continued exploring various strategies. I won’t say explore, I actually went into search mode on YouTube and X also. I found TikTok’s search function to be very limited for discovering high quality, high merit content. But on X, you could find actual traders, not just influencers, and you could see authentic posts and replies from them. 


## Variations of CRT - Romeo and Others
Romeo is credited as being the founder of CRT. His videos are not public though, unless the mentorship sessions are leaked by another YouTube account. Others also preach about CRT but in a general sense. I could not find live trades or trade recaps. 

Some CRTs are on the 1 hour candle, usually at 9AM. Others are a daily candle or H4. The methods for entry also vary. SMT is a common confluence used, then key levels, ICT structures like order blocks, fair value gaps, inverted FVGs, breaker blocks, all these also are common confluences apart from the basic turtle soup raid. It is easy to get into the mix of strategies offered and get either distracted or overwhelmed. I could not find consistency and a sustainable system in the array of CRT strategies thrown.

## Hancock’s Model
This is probably the only well documented CRT strategy I’ve come across. It was because of his documented setups that I found it easy to verify, to follow, and to emulate well. I started demo trading on MT5 and was very confident so I also live traded. Because my trade reviews required an R:R number I started using TradingView; and yes, TradingView is such an incredible trading tool; even the free version.

But like all trading strategies, Hancock’s method wasn’t without flaw. The first problem was to do with CSD2 identification. His method was dependent on a purge of CSD1 before CSD2 can be validated. There were times when the purge never happened, but the move happened. There were other times when the purge happened but went beyond, so you could have 3 or 4 CSDs before the correct one happened at a certain level.

When Gold started trading at all time highs I stumbled upon PO3 numbers. These helped to identify key levels that seemed to be respected. This was accurate for me; but initially I did not know how to differentiate markings for PO3 values of order 27, 81, 243, 729, etc. It is only recently that I’ve seen that differentiating these can be helpful. Since one PO3 level will have 3 partitions corresponding to discount, equilibrium, and premium levels.

Let me explain the second issue with Hancock’s method. His 9AM model is  reversal model. But he also built a continuation model. This model though, is not defined well. Yes, it does state a CSD requirement, but it is unclear whether it requires a small reversal move within the range, or a breakout from the purge onwards, it is just not defined well to cover distinct scenarios.

The scenarios are:
A purge then a retracement to 50% CR then continuation
A purge then a retracement to 95% CR then continuation
A breakout of CR and continuation without retracing into the CR
A breakout of CR and retracement to CR, then continuation 

At some point, I questioned if the last H4 candle range was valid. Sometimes it was a CRT on three H1 candles that was playing out, other times, an 8 hour range was playing out, or a 2 hour range. So are H4 ranges valid and to be kept as rigid markers?

Timed CRT - 1, 9 or 1, 5, 9
With Hancock, the 1 and 9 timings were emphasized. They still are. 9AM will always be the key timing. But as I mentioned, a reversal is not always guaranteed. There are many continuation days.

I tried trading 1AM and 9AM, then 9PM,1AM, 9AM, then I also started checking 1PM. It’s just not every day you get a setup, and it is not worth giving so much time. I’ve forced setups a good number of times. I’ve lost a lot of capital carelessly forcing setups.

## Neo’s 911 Model
It was around the same time I wanted to explore my own timed ranges. Instead of the 4 hour timed ranged used by Hancock, i wanted longer ranges. I called it Neo’s 911 Model. 9AM to 1PM. 1PM to 1AM. The 4H ranges of 1 and 9 were just not sufficient, so I wanted to see if longer timed ranges were workable. The ranges were:
9AM-1PM - Trade
1PM-1AM - Trade
1AM-9AM - Observe

I was just trading liquidity and simply moving from highs to lows. I never developed it consistently. I just lost a lot of money trying it on my live account so I returned to Hancock’s method. I should’ve demo tested it out.

## Adding PO3 to Hancock’s Model
In the time I returned, I stumbled upon PO3 levels. And this to me, is going to be a permanent part of my trading because I think i have seen without a shadow of a doubt that these levels are respected. I’ve seen the expansion levels to be freakishly accurate on Bitcoin and that is enough to convince me of it.

I did one trade on Gold with a PO3 level being respected at all time highs. But after that, I have not been consistent with accurate setups.

Hancock’s CSDs require a key level. And many times it is difficult to identify that key level. But i have seen that PO3 levels especially those of a higher degree give high probability levels for these reversals. Usually, an ICT structure is found at these to confirm it is a key level.

## Adding Fibonacci to Hancock’s Model
Last night, while lying in bed, I pondered about this combination. What if I add fib levels to Hancock’s method for retracements to 50% CR then continuations?

Now I have the PO3 markings so I will be able to understand discount, premium, and equilibrium levels based on just PO3 levels.

So I am thinking say if price was bullish and in a discount partition then it is more likely we will see a retracement to the PO3 level then a continuation towards eq and premium. But if it was bullish and in a premium partition, a reversal will be more likely there. (This is an interesting idea that is worth backtesting)

The fib levels were an additional tool I wanted to use, since price tends to retrace to 61.8 before continuing the impulsive move.

## Overtrading Multiple Timings and Multiple Instruments and Multiple Accounts
Overtrading is nothing new to me. Recently I’ve overtraded key times for 1,5,9 then again with Gold, US30, NAS100, and BTCUSD. It’s really a lot. But I am coming to see that quantity is not what makes profit. Quality is. Overtrading in multiple accounts is a similar issue. For me, using one account will bring more focus. 

We need to cut out this dissipation of focus and now start to look at just one asset, one timing, and one account. Of course one strategy is needed, but i am still developing that. 

We have the scaling plan already with a 20-trade cycle. That should be sufficient. The only issue is setups will not come every day.
